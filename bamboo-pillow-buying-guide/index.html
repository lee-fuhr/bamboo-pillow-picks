How to choose bamboo pillows

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

To choose a bamboo pillow, look for a shredded memory foam fill that lets you adjust the loft, a breathable bamboo-derived cover, and a construction that promotes airflow so you don’t sleep hot. Focus on your sleep position: side sleepers usually need a higher loft, back sleepers a medium one, and stomach sleepers a low one. The best pick will also have a removable, washable cover and a generous return policy in case the feel isn’t right.

What to look for in a bamboo pillow

Start with the fill. Shredded memory foam is the most popular choice because you can pull out or add pieces to dial in the height (loft) and firmness. This adjustability is a major advantage over solid memory foam pillows, which are fixed after they’re foamed. Next, check the cover. A genuine “bamboo” cover is usually a blend of rayon made from bamboo fibers and polyester. It should feel soft, smooth, and breathable. Look for covers labeled “removable” and “machine washable”. this makes cleaning much easier. Avoid pillows where the cover is permanently attached, because the pillow itself will trap sweat and skin cells over time.

Shredded memory foam vs solid memory foam pillow

Solid memory foam pillows are molded into a single piece. They offer consistent support but can’t be adjusted. Many people find them too high or too firm. Shredded memory foam pillows are filled with small pieces that compress and move independently, which lets you reshape the pillow and remove or add fill to suit your preferred height. Shredded foam also tends to sleep cooler because there are air gaps between the pieces. Solid foam can trap heat and feel hot at night. If you tend to sleep warm, a shredded fill with a breathable bamboo cover is a much safer bet.

How loft and firmness affect your sleep position

Loft, how thick the pillow is, is the single most important factor for alignment. Side sleepers generally need a high loft (several inches of thickness) to fill the gap between your ear and the mattress. Back sleepers need a medium loft that supports the curve of the neck without tilting the head forward. Stomach sleepers need a low loft, sometimes just a thin layer, to keep the spine neutral. Firmness is tied to fill density. Shredded memory foam pillows come in different densities. A denser fill will feel more supportive and have less give; a lighter fill will feel softer and more moldable. The ideal combination lets your head sink in slightly without the pillow flattening completely under your weight.

Breathability and cooling features

Bamboo-derived fabric is naturally more breathable than standard cotton or polyester because the fibers have microscopic gaps that wick moisture away. But the cover is only one part of the cooling equation. The fill matters more: shredded memory foam allows air to circulate between the pieces, which reduces heat buildup compared to solid foam. Some pillows also add a cooling gel layer or infuse the foam with charcoal or graphite particles to draw heat away. These can help, but they’re not magic. The simplest test is to look at user feedback: if a pillow is widely praised as “cool”, it’s probably doing something right. If complaints about heat are common, skip it.

Washing and care for bamboo pillows

Most bamboo pillows with shredded memory foam have a removable zippered cover that can be machine washed on a gentle cycle and tumble dried low. The foam fill itself should not go in the washing machine, it absorbs water like a sponge and will take days to dry, and the tumbling can tear the pieces. To refresh the foam, spot clean with a mild detergent and water, then let it air dry thoroughly. You can also fluff the pillow in a dryer on no heat with a couple of clean tennis balls to redistribite the filling. Plan to replace the pillow every 2–3 years, or whenever it no longer bounces back to shape.

Is a bamboo cover really better than cotton or polyester?

A bamboo-derived rayon cover is softer than most cotton weaves and wicks moisture better than plain polyester. It also tends to be more wrinkle-resistant and has a silky feel that many sleepers prefer against the face. However, “bamboo” is a marketing term, the fabric is actually rayon made from bamboo pulp, not raw bamboo fibers woven together. That doesn’t make it bad, just be aware it’s a semi-synthetic fabric. What matters more than the cover material is the weave. A tencel or lyocell weave (sometimes made from eucalyptus) can be just as breathable. If sustainability is important, look for a certified organic cotton cover or a pillow whose bamboo rayon is made with a closed-loop manufacturing process. For pure comfort, a well-made bamboo cover is excellent.

Frequently asked questions

Are bamboo pillows good for side sleepers?

Yes, they can be excellent for side sleepers because shredded memory foam allows you to add or remove fill to get the high loft needed to keep your spine straight. Look for a pillow that comes with extra fill or lets you customize the height easily.

How long do shredded memory foam pillows last?

Most good-quality shredded memory foam pillows last between two and three years with regular use. Over time the foam pieces can lose their resilience and flatten. If your pillow no longer springs back after fluffing or you wake up with neck pain, it’s time to replace it.

Can I adjust the amount of fill in a bamboo pillow?

Many shredded memory foam bamboo pillows have a zippered compartment that lets you remove or add foam pieces. Some pillows also come with a bag of extra fill so you can gradually increase the loft. Always check the product description to confirm adjustability before buying.

Are bamboo pillows machine washable?

Typically only the outer cover is machine washable. The shredded memory foam fill should be hand-washed or spot-cleaned and air-dried. Always remove the cover and wash it on a gentle cycle with mild detergent. Never put the foam inside a washing machine or dryer.

Do bamboo pillows have a chemical smell?

Some memory foam pillows have a temporary “off-gassing” smell when first opened, especially if sealed in plastic packaging. Shredded foam generally off-gasses less than solid foam because there’s more surface area exposed to air. Airing the pillow for 24–48 hours usually eliminates the odor. A high-quality bamboo cover can also help mask any initial smell.

Are bamboo pillows hypoallergenic?

Bamboo-derived rayon fabric is naturally resistant to dust mites and mold because it wicks moisture away. The shredded memory foam itself is not inherently hypoallergenic, but many brands add antimicrobial treatments or use CertiPUR-US certified foam that has low VOC emissions. If you have severe allergies, look for a pillow that specifically states it is hypoallergenic or uses a tightly woven cover that keeps out allergens.

In shortChoosing a bamboo pillow isn’t complicated once you know what matters: adjustable shredded memory foam, a breathable and washable cover, and a loft that matches your sleeping position. Don’t get distracted by flashy claims, focus on the fill and the freedom to customize. A good bamboo pillow will improve your sleep quality right away and hold up for years with basic care.
